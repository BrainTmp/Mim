---
title: Mim - The Well of Knowledge
---
> Where shadowed waters gnaw the World-Tree's bones,  
> Óðinn the Hooded One knelt—alone.  
> _"Wisdom I crave, though the price be mine eye!"_  
> But Mímir's cold voice hissed through the sigh:  
> _"Nay, Allfather. Not sight, but_ _**joy's bright thread**_—  
> _Three and nine, and two I wed_  
> _To thy name forever. Drink, and know_  
> _What gnaws the hearts where rune-winds blow."_

> He drank the black silence. No scream tore the air,  
> Yet his mirth drowned deep in that liquid despair.  
> No more does he feast where the mead-horns ring,  
> No more does he laugh where the skalds sing—  
> For the **well's wisdom-weight** on his brow lies cold,  
> A _numbered ghost_ in his soul takes hold.

> Wanderer who treads this well-spring of knowing:  
> Touch these runes at thy own foreshowing.  
> Drink deep as Óðinn—gain sight that sees far—  
> But mourn like the Allfather: _his joy drowned..._  
> _...his Rune-Score burns with_ _**three-point-nine-twó.**_
